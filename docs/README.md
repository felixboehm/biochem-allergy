# Allergy Biochemistry Research — Documentation Index

## Overview

This documentation maps the complete biochemical landscape from food intake through hormonal response, nutrient metabolism, inflammation, allergy, and medication effects. The documents are designed to be read in sequence but can also be used as standalone references.

## Documents

### 1. [Hormonal & Biochemical Cascade of Food Intake](hormonal-biochemical-cascade-food-intake.md)
The starting point: what happens biochemically when you eat.
- Cephalic phase (vagus nerve, anticipatory insulin)
- Digestive hormones (ghrelin, CCK, GLP-1, GIP, PYY, secretin, gastrin)
- Insulin/glucagon axis (IRS → PI3K → Akt → GLUT4; cAMP/PKA gluconeogenesis)
- Nutrient sensing (mTORC1, AMPK, FGF21)
- Satiety (leptin/adiponectin → hypothalamic circuits)
- Cortisol and thyroid hormone modulation of metabolism

### 2. [Optimal Nutrient Requirements & Inflammation Impact](nutrient-requirements-and-inflammation.md)

- Macronutrients (protein/amino acids, carbs/fiber, fats/omega balance)
- Minerals (Mg, Zn, Fe, Se, Ca, K, Cu) — biochemical roles, enzyme specifics
- Vitamins (D, B-complex, C, A, E) — pathways and cofactor roles
- Quantitative daily requirements table (RDA vs optimal vs upper limit)
- Inflammation-driven depletion mechanisms per nutrient
- Nutrient synergies and antagonisms (interaction matrix)

### 3. [Inflammation Biochemistry & Nutrient Depletion Cycles](inflammation-biochemistry-nutrient-cycles.md)
How inflammation becomes chronic and self-sustaining through nutrient depletion.
- Acute inflammation (NF-κB, COX/LOX, cytokines, resolution mediators)
- Chronic "stuck states" (failed resolution, NLRP3, ROS loops, mitochondrial dysfunction)
- Nutrient depletion vicious cycles (Mg, Zn, vitamin D, B vitamins, Fe, Se)
- Absorption impairment catch-22s (Mg needs Mg to absorb Mg)
- Metabolic pathway disruption (Krebs cycle, ETC, methylation, glutathione, tryptophan)
- **Symptom → biochemical state mapping** (fatigue, brain fog, cramps, anxiety, insomnia, skin, infections, GI, joint pain, histamine intolerance)

### 4. [Allergy, Inflammation, Nutrition & Medications](allergy-inflammation-nutrition-medications.md)
How allergy layers onto all of the above, and how treatments create new problems.
- IgE-mediated hypersensitivity (sensitization → degranulation cascade)
- Mast cell mediators (histamine, tryptase, PGD2, leukotrienes, PAF)
- Allergy-driven chronic inflammation (Th2 skewing, eosinophil damage, barrier dysfunction)
- Allergy's direct effect on nutrient status (DAO cofactor depletion, dietary restriction gaps)
- Glucocorticoid treatment: mechanism + metabolic side effects (glucose, bone, Mg, K, vitamin D, Zn)
- Antihistamines: H1 blockers, H2 blockers (nutrient absorption impairment), montelukast, cromolyn
- **The combined picture**: convergent nutrient depletion map for allergy + cortisol + antihistamines

### 5. [Allergy-to-NARE Transition](allergy-to-nare-transition.md)
Why symptoms persist after pollen disappears — the mechanistic shift from IgE-driven to eosinophil-driven disease.
- Epithelial reprogramming: alarmin (TSLP/IL-33/IL-25) → ILC2 axis replaces IgE/Th2
- Barrier damage letting irritants become new triggers
- Neurogenic inflammation lock-in (TRPV1/TRPA1, substance P/CGRP)
- Eosinophil self-sustaining loops (PGD2/DP2/CRTH2 feedback)
- Microbiome shift perpetuating inflammation
- **Diagnostic distinction table**: post-allergy lingering vs NARE
- Diagnostic workup (nasal cytology, blood markers)
- Progression risk: NARE → nasal polyps → asthma → Samter's triad
